--- Workflow Specification (Textual Description) ---

This workflow describes how an instructor creates a course and manages it
over time. An authenticated instructor accesses the instructor dashboard
which shows a summary of their created courses and enrolled student counts.
From there, the instructor creates a new course by filling in the course
title, description, category, and difficulty level. The instructor then
organizes the course into modules, and adds video lectures to each module by
providing a title, description, and video URL. The instructor can also
attach supplementary resources such as PDFs and slides to each lecture.
Additionally, the instructor creates quizzes for each module by adding
multiple-choice questions and setting a passing score. Once all content is
added, the instructor publishes the course to make it visible to students in
the catalog. After publication, the instructor can view basic enrollment
statistics, update course content, and edit quiz questions.

--- Workflow Description (Step-by-Step) ---

Step 1: Accessing the Instructor Dashboard
   - The instructor navigates to their dashboard.
   - The dashboard displays: a list of their created courses, the number of
     enrolled students per course, and overall course status (draft or
     published).
   - The instructor clicks "Create New Course" to begin.

Step 2: Entering Course Information
   - The system presents a course creation form.
   - The instructor fills in: course title, course description, category
     (e.g., Web Development, Data Science, Graphic Design), and difficulty
     level (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced).
   - The instructor optionally uploads a course thumbnail image.
   - The instructor clicks "Save & Continue" to proceed to adding content.

Step 3: Creating Modules
   - The system displays a course content builder page.
   - The instructor clicks "Add Module" to create a new module/section.
   - For each module, the instructor enters a module title and a brief
     description.
   - The instructor can add multiple modules to organize the course
     logically (e.g., Module 1: Introduction, Module 2: Core Concepts).
   - Modules are displayed in the order they are added; the instructor can
     change the order number to rearrange them.

Step 4: Adding Video Lectures to Modules
   - Within each module, the instructor clicks "Add Lecture."
   - For each lecture, the instructor provides: a lecture title, a brief
     description, and a video URL (e.g., a link to an uploaded video file
     or an external video hosting service).
   - The instructor can add multiple lectures per module.
   - Lectures appear in order within their module; the instructor can
     adjust the order number as needed.

Step 5: Attaching Supplementary Resources
   - For each lecture, the instructor can click "Add Resource."
   - The instructor uploads a file (PDF, slides, or code files) and gives
     it a descriptive title.
   - Each resource is associated with the specific lecture it belongs to.
   - Students will be able to download these resources when viewing the
     lecture.

Step 6: Creating Quizzes for Modules
   - Within each module, the instructor clicks "Add Quiz."
   - The instructor provides a quiz title and sets a passing score
     percentage (e.g., 70%).
   - The instructor adds questions one by one. For each question:
     * The instructor types the question text.
     * The instructor adds 2 to 4 answer options.
     * The instructor marks which option is the correct answer.
   - The instructor can add as many questions as needed.
   - The instructor saves the quiz, and it is linked to the module.

Step 7: Publishing the Course
   - After adding all modules, lectures, resources, and quizzes, the
     instructor clicks "Publish Course."
   - The system checks that the course has at least one module with at
     least one lecture before allowing publication.
   - Upon publishing, the course status changes from "Draft" to "Published"
     and the course becomes visible in the student course catalog.

Step 8: Managing the Course After Publication
   - The instructor can view basic statistics from the dashboard: total
     number of students enrolled in each course.
   - The instructor can edit existing course content at any time:
     * Update the course title, description, or category.
     * Add new modules or lectures.
     * Edit or delete existing quiz questions.
     * Upload additional resources.
   - Changes are reflected immediately for enrolled students.

--- Key Features & Functionalities ---

   - Instructor dashboard with course list and enrollment counts
   - Course creation form with title, description, category, difficulty
   - Module-based course organization with ordering
   - Video lecture addition via URL with title and description
   - Supplementary resource file upload per lecture
   - Quiz builder with multiple-choice questions and passing score
   - Course publishing with basic validation
   - Post-publication course editing and content updates
   - Basic enrollment statistics per course
