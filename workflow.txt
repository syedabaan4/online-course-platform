================================================================================
                        ONLINE COURSE PLATFORM
                      Workflow Specification Document
================================================================================

Project Description:
Online Course Platform where instructors can create and manage courses with
video lectures, quizzes, and resources, and students can enroll, learn at
their own pace, and earn certificates.

Note: User authentication (registration, login, signup) is assumed to be a
given and is not included in these workflow descriptions.

================================================================================
                         WORKFLOW 1
             Course Browsing, Enrollment & Learning
             (Member 1 - Group Leader - Syed Abaan)
================================================================================


--- Workflow Specification (Textual Description) ---

This workflow covers the student's journey of discovering courses, enrolling
in them, and actively learning. An authenticated student navigates to the
course catalog where they can browse all available courses, search by
keywords, and filter by category or difficulty level. The student can view a
detailed course page showing the syllabus, instructor name, course
description, and list of modules. When the student finds a course they want,
they enroll in it. After enrollment, the student accesses the course player
where they can watch video lectures module by module, download supplementary
resources (PDFs, slides, code files), and mark lectures as complete. The
system tracks the student's progress with a completion percentage so they
can resume learning at any time from where they left off.

--- Workflow Description (Step-by-Step) ---

Step 1: Browsing the Course Catalog
   - The student navigates to the "Browse Courses" page from the dashboard.
   - The system displays a catalog of all available courses as cards showing
     the course thumbnail, title, instructor name, and category.
   - The student can use the search bar to search by course title or keyword.
   - The student can apply filters: category (e.g., Programming, Design,
     Business) and difficulty level (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced).
   - The filtered or searched results update on the page accordingly.

Step 2: Viewing Course Details
   - The student clicks on a course card to view the full course detail page.
   - The course detail page displays: course title, detailed description,
     instructor name, course category, difficulty level, total number of
     modules and lectures, and a full syllabus listing all module titles
     and their lecture titles.
   - The student reviews this information to decide whether to enroll.

Step 3: Enrolling in a Course
   - The student clicks the "Enroll Now" button on the course detail page.
   - The system enrolls the student and displays a success confirmation
     message.
   - The enrolled course now appears in the student's "My Courses" section
     on their dashboard.
   - The student can enroll in multiple courses simultaneously.

Step 4: Accessing Course Content & Watching Lectures
   - The student navigates to "My Courses" and clicks on an enrolled course.
   - The system displays the course player interface with: a video player in
     the main area, and a sidebar listing all modules and their lectures
     with checkmarks indicating completion status.
   - The student clicks on a lecture to begin watching the video.
   - The student can pause, play, rewind, and fast-forward the video using
     standard HTML5 video controls.
   - After finishing a lecture, the student clicks "Mark as Complete" to
     update their progress.

Step 5: Downloading Resources
   - Below the video player, the student can see a "Resources" section
     listing all downloadable files attached to the current lecture (PDFs,
     slides, code files, etc.).
   - The student clicks the download button next to a resource to save it
     locally.

Step 6: Tracking Progress & Resuming
   - A progress bar at the top of the course player shows the student's
     overall course completion percentage (based on how many lectures are
     marked complete out of the total).
   - The student's progress is saved automatically in the database.
   - When the student returns later, they go to "My Courses" and click
     "Continue Learning." The system takes them directly to the first
     incomplete lecture.
   - The dashboard shows all enrolled courses with their respective
     completion percentages at a glance.

--- Key Features & Functionalities ---

   - Course catalog with search and filter (by category, difficulty)
   - Detailed course pages with full syllabus
   - One-click course enrollment
   - Video lecture player with standard playback controls
   - Module and lecture sidebar with completion indicators
   - Downloadable supplementary resources per lecture
   - Progress tracking with visual completion percentage
   - Resume learning from last incomplete lecture


================================================================================
                         WORKFLOW 2
            Instructor Course Creation & Management
                         (Member 2)
================================================================================

--- Workflow Specification (Textual Description) ---

This workflow describes how an instructor creates a course and manages it
over time. An authenticated instructor accesses the instructor dashboard
which shows a summary of their created courses and enrolled student counts.
From there, the instructor creates a new course by filling in the course
title, description, category, and difficulty level. The instructor then
organizes the course into modules, and adds video lectures to each module by
providing a title, description, and video URL. The instructor can also
attach supplementary resources such as PDFs and slides to each lecture.
Additionally, the instructor creates quizzes for each module by adding
multiple-choice questions and setting a passing score. Once all content is
added, the instructor publishes the course to make it visible to students in
the catalog. After publication, the instructor can view basic enrollment
statistics, update course content, and edit quiz questions.

--- Workflow Description (Step-by-Step) ---

Step 1: Accessing the Instructor Dashboard
   - The instructor navigates to their dashboard.
   - The dashboard displays: a list of their created courses, the number of
     enrolled students per course, and overall course status (draft or
     published).
   - The instructor clicks "Create New Course" to begin.

Step 2: Entering Course Information
   - The system presents a course creation form.
   - The instructor fills in: course title, course description, category
     (e.g., Web Development, Data Science, Graphic Design), and difficulty
     level (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced).
   - The instructor optionally uploads a course thumbnail image.
   - The instructor clicks "Save & Continue" to proceed to adding content.

Step 3: Creating Modules
   - The system displays a course content builder page.
   - The instructor clicks "Add Module" to create a new module/section.
   - For each module, the instructor enters a module title and a brief
     description.
   - The instructor can add multiple modules to organize the course
     logically (e.g., Module 1: Introduction, Module 2: Core Concepts).
   - Modules are displayed in the order they are added; the instructor can
     change the order number to rearrange them.

Step 4: Adding Video Lectures to Modules
   - Within each module, the instructor clicks "Add Lecture."
   - For each lecture, the instructor provides: a lecture title, a brief
     description, and a video URL (e.g., a link to an uploaded video file
     or an external video hosting service).
   - The instructor can add multiple lectures per module.
   - Lectures appear in order within their module; the instructor can
     adjust the order number as needed.

Step 5: Attaching Supplementary Resources
   - For each lecture, the instructor can click "Add Resource."
   - The instructor uploads a file (PDF, slides, or code files) and gives
     it a descriptive title.
   - Each resource is associated with the specific lecture it belongs to.
   - Students will be able to download these resources when viewing the
     lecture.

Step 6: Creating Quizzes for Modules
   - Within each module, the instructor clicks "Add Quiz."
   - The instructor provides a quiz title and sets a passing score
     percentage (e.g., 70%).
   - The instructor adds questions one by one. For each question:
     * The instructor types the question text.
     * The instructor adds 2 to 4 answer options.
     * The instructor marks which option is the correct answer.
   - The instructor can add as many questions as needed.
   - The instructor saves the quiz, and it is linked to the module.

Step 7: Publishing the Course
   - After adding all modules, lectures, resources, and quizzes, the
     instructor clicks "Publish Course."
   - The system checks that the course has at least one module with at
     least one lecture before allowing publication.
   - Upon publishing, the course status changes from "Draft" to "Published"
     and the course becomes visible in the student course catalog.

Step 8: Managing the Course After Publication
   - The instructor can view basic statistics from the dashboard: total
     number of students enrolled in each course.
   - The instructor can edit existing course content at any time:
     * Update the course title, description, or category.
     * Add new modules or lectures.
     * Edit or delete existing quiz questions.
     * Upload additional resources.
   - Changes are reflected immediately for enrolled students.

--- Key Features & Functionalities ---

   - Instructor dashboard with course list and enrollment counts
   - Course creation form with title, description, category, difficulty
   - Module-based course organization with ordering
   - Video lecture addition via URL with title and description
   - Supplementary resource file upload per lecture
   - Quiz builder with multiple-choice questions and passing score
   - Course publishing with basic validation
   - Post-publication course editing and content updates
   - Basic enrollment statistics per course


================================================================================
                         WORKFLOW 3
          Quiz Attempt, Grading & Certificate Generation
                         (Member 3)
================================================================================

--- Workflow Specification (Textual Description) ---

This workflow covers the process of a student taking quizzes within a
course, receiving grades and feedback, and earning a certificate upon course
completion. As the student progresses through course modules and completes
lectures, they encounter quizzes at the end of each module. The student
starts a quiz, answers all multiple-choice questions, and submits it for
grading. The system automatically evaluates the answers, calculates the
score, and presents the results with a breakdown showing which questions
were correct and which were wrong. If the student achieves the passing
score, the module quiz is marked as passed; otherwise, the student may
reattempt the quiz. Once the student has completed all lectures and passed
all module quizzes in a course, the system recognizes the course as fully
completed. The student can then generate a certificate of completion which
is displayed on screen with their name, course title, completion date, and
a unique certificate ID, and can be downloaded as a PDF.

--- Workflow Description (Step-by-Step) ---

Step 1: Navigating to a Module Quiz
   - The student is progressing through a course and has completed the
     video lectures within a module.
   - At the end of the module, the sidebar displays a "Module Quiz" item.
   - The student clicks on the quiz to open the quiz information page.
   - The quiz information page displays: quiz title, number of questions,
     and the passing score requirement (e.g., 70%).
   - The student clicks "Start Quiz" to begin the attempt.

Step 2: Answering Quiz Questions
   - The system loads the quiz interface displaying all questions on the
     page.
   - For each multiple-choice question, the student selects one answer from
     the provided options by clicking the corresponding radio button.
   - A counter shows how many questions have been answered out of the total
     (e.g., "5 of 10 answered").
   - The student works through all questions at their own pace.

Step 3: Submitting the Quiz
   - After answering all questions, the student clicks "Submit Quiz."
   - A confirmation dialog appears: "Are you sure you want to submit?"
   - The student confirms, and the system accepts the submission for
     grading.

Step 4: Automated Grading & Score Calculation
   - The system compares each submitted answer against the correct answer
     stored in the database.
   - The system calculates: total correct answers, total incorrect answers,
     and the overall score as a percentage.
   - The system determines whether the student has passed or failed based
     on the passing score threshold set by the instructor.

Step 5: Viewing Quiz Results & Feedback
   - The system displays the results page showing: the student's score
     (e.g., "8 out of 10 — 80%") and pass/fail status highlighted in
     green for pass or red for fail.
   - Below the summary, a question-by-question breakdown is shown:
     * Each question is listed with the student's selected answer and the
       correct answer.
     * Correctly answered questions are marked with a green checkmark.
     * Incorrectly answered questions are marked with a red cross, and the
       correct answer is revealed.
   - The student reviews this feedback to understand their mistakes.

Step 6: Reattempting a Failed Quiz
   - If the student did not achieve the passing score, a "Retake Quiz"
     button is displayed on the results page.
   - The student clicks "Retake Quiz" to start a new attempt.
   - The student goes through the same process of answering and submitting.
   - The system stores the latest attempt's score.
   - The student can reattempt the quiz as many times as needed until they
     pass.

Step 7: Course Completion Check
   - As the student passes each module quiz and marks all lectures as
     complete, the system tracks overall course progress.
   - The system checks two conditions for course completion:
     * All video lectures across all modules are marked as complete.
     * All module quizzes have been passed (score >= passing threshold).
   - Once both conditions are met, the system updates the course status
     to "Completed" on the student's dashboard.
   - A congratulatory message is shown: "Congratulations! You have
     completed [Course Title]!"

Step 8: Certificate Generation & Download
   - On the completed course page, a "Get Certificate" button becomes
     available.
   - The student clicks "Get Certificate."
   - The system generates a certificate containing:
     * The student's full name.
     * The course title.
     * The instructor's name.
     * The date of completion.
     * A unique Certificate ID (e.g., CERT-2025-XXXXXX).
   - The certificate is displayed on screen as a styled certificate view.
   - The student clicks "Download PDF" to save the certificate as a PDF
     file to their device.
   - The certificate is also stored in the student's profile under a
     "My Certificates" section, so they can access it again at any time.

--- Key Features & Functionalities ---

   - Module-end quizzes with multiple-choice questions
   - Automated grading with instant score calculation
   - Pass/fail determination based on instructor-set threshold
   - Detailed results with question-by-question feedback
   - Quiz reattempt functionality for failed attempts
   - Automatic course completion detection (lectures + quizzes)
   - Certificate generation with student name, course, date, and unique ID
   - Certificate download as PDF
   - Persistent certificate storage in student profile
